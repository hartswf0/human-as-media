The Viral Editor can eat anything.

  • Visibility is governed by [ranking] and [amplifying].

MIROSHNICHENKO'S CHALLENGE:

"How does your medium survive in an ecosystem where algorithms steer attention, not truth?"

Power Questions

<Monopoly-over-Meaning>
Does the tool [decentralize] meaning or [recentralize] it in new elites?

Victor wants to kill the 'priesthood' of static symbols. Miro asks if he is simply creating a new priesthood of 'Simulation Architects'—those capable of building the models everyone else lives inside.

Power Questions

<Internet-Presence-as-Existence>
Does the tool [relieve] the obligation to publish—or [intensify] it?

If 'drawing dynamic visualizations' becomes the standard for truth, does the burden of expression become too heavy for the average user? 'What is possible becomes obligatory.'

Power Questions

<Temporal-Stress>
Does better instrumentation [reduce] stress—or [accelerate] the cycle of novelty?

Victor argues that seeing the system reduces the stress of not knowing. Miro warns that real-time feedback loops often increase anxiety and the speed of reaction, destroying the time needed for 'thinking.'

Power Questions

<Institutions-versus-Net>
Can the tool [reconcile] institutional cohesion with network freedom?

Victor is essentially an institutionalist building tools for individuals. Can these tools create shared reality (Institutions) or just fractured, personal realities (The Net)?
